ACADEMIC TRACK

STEM

(Science Technology Engineering Mathematics)

STEM is an educational approach that focuses on integrating these four disciplines to promote critical thinking, problem-solving, and innovation. STEM education seeks to equip students with the knowledge and skills to tackle real-world challenges and prepare them for careers in fields such as engineering, computer science, healthcare, and environmental science, among others. The STEM approach involves hands-on learning experiences, project-based activities, and inquiry-based instruction that encourages students to explore and experiment with concepts and ideas. STEM education emphasizes the use of technology and other tools to collect and analyze data, design and build prototypes, and test and evaluate solutions to problems.

HUMSS

(Humanities and Social Sciences)

HUMSS stands for Humanities and Social Sciences Strand, which is a senior high school track offered in the Philippines. It is one of the four tracks in the Academic Track, which also includes the STEM, ABM, and General Academic strands. HUMSS is designed for students who are interested in pursuing careers in fields related to social sciences, humanities, and communication, such as education, law, journalism, psychology, sociology, history, and political science, among others. It aims to provide students with a strong foundation in critical thinking, research, communication, and social awareness. The HUMSS curriculum includes subjects such as Philippine Politics and Governance, Creative Writing, Introduction to World Religions and Belief Systems, Disciplines and Ideas in the Social Sciences, and Understanding Culture, Society, and Politics, among others.

GAS

(General Academic Strand)

GAS stands for "General Academic Strand," which means that students who opt for this track will have a broad and well-rounded academic experience that covers a range of subjects. This strand is ideal for students who are interested in pursuing college degrees in a variety of fields, including law, business, communication, education, and other non-science courses. Students taking the GAS Strand will study various subjects such as economics, psychology, history, sociology, philosophy, literature, and languages. The GAS Strand curriculum also includes mathematics courses such as calculus, statistics, and probability, as well as science courses like biology, chemistry, and physics. One of the benefits of the GAS Strand is that it gives students the flexibility to explore their interests and develop skills that are relevant to different career paths.
