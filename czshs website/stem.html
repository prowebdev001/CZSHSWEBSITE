ACADEMIC TRACK

STEM

(Science Technology Engineering Mathematics)

      The STEM strand refers to a specific academic track or strand in the education system, usually at the high school level. STEM stands for Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics, and the STEM strand is designed to provide students with a comprehensive foundation in these subjects. The STEM strand aims to develop students' critical thinking, problem-solving, and analytical skills by focusing on the disciplines of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics.

      They learn how to apply scientific principles, utilize technology, and employ mathematical concepts to solve real-world problems. The curriculum may cover topics such as physics, chemistry, biology, computer programming, robotics, calculus, and statistics, among others. The STEM strand also places a strong emphasis on innovation, creativity, and collaboration.It can lead to diverse career paths, such as becoming engineers, scientists, programmers, researchers, data analysts, mathematicians, and professionals in various technical fields.
